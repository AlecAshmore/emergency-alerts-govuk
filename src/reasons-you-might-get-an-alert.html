Reasons you might get an emergency alert

Emergency Alerts are being developed by the UK government and mobile network operators.

If thereâ€™s a test in your local area, you might get an emergency alert.

You may also receive an alert if you travel through a test area during a test.

After we launch Emergency Alerts

Emergency Alerts will be used to warn the public about:

  • severe flooding
  • fires
  • explosions
  • terrorist incidents
  • public health emergencies

Only the emergency services and authorised government agencies will be able to send alerts.

For most people, the chance of receiving an emergency alert is low.

How often you receive an alert will depend on where you live or work. For example, if you spend a lot of time in an area with a high risk of severe flooding, you may get more than one alert a year.
