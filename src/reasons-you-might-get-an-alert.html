Reasons you might get an emergency alert

If thereâ€™s a danger to life in the local area, you might get an emergency alert.

You may also receive occasional test alerts, to make sure the system is working.

Emergency alerts can be used to warn the public about:

  • severe flooding
  • fires
  • explosions
  • terrorist incidents
  • public health emergencies

Only the emergency services and authorised government agencies can send alerts.

For most people, the chance of receiving an emergency alert is low.

How often you receive an alert depends on where you live or work. For example, if you spend a lot of time in an area with a high risk of severe flooding, you may get more than one alert a year.
