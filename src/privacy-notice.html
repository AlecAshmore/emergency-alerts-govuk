Privacy notice: Emergency Alerts

This privacy notice relates to:

  • Emergency Alerts
  • gov.uk/alerts
  • the Emergency Alerts feedback survey

It explains what personal data we collect and what we do with it. It also explains your rights and how to enforce them.

Who manages this service

The Government Digital Service (GDS), part of the Cabinet Office, is working with partners across central government, local government and mobile network operators (MNOs) to deliver the Emergency Alerts service.

The owner of the Emergency Alerts service is the Department for Digital, Culture, Media & Sport (DCMS).

The Cabinet Office is the data controller for any personal data processed on gov.uk/alerts and the feedback survey.

A data controller determines how and why personal data is processed. For more information, read the Cabinet Office’s entry in the Data Protection Public Register.

What personal data we collect

Emergency Alerts

Emergency alerts are used to warn people when there’s a life-threatening event nearby.

They can be sent by:

  • the emergency services
  • government departments, agencies and public bodies that deal with emergencies

Emergency alerts are broadcast from mobile phone masts to every compatible phone and tablet within range.

The sender does not need to know your mobile phone number or any other personal data to send you an alert.

No one will collect or share data about you, your phone or your location when you receive an emergency alert.

For more information, see how emergency alerts work.

gov.uk/alerts

When you visit gov.uk/alerts, we collect your Internet Protocol (IP) address, and details of which operating system and web browser you used.

We do not use cookies or page tagging to collect information about how you use the site.

Emergency Alerts feedback survey

The UK government is still testing Emergency Alerts. If you are in a test area and receive a test alert, you may want to complete the feedback survey.

The survey asks questions about your experience of Emergency Alerts.

Question 7 asks you to enter your email address. This question is optional.

The rest of the questions do not ask you for personal information. Please do not enter anything that could be used to identify you - for example, your name, address or phone number.

Why we need your data

We collect information so that we can:

  • let users access government services and make transactions
  • gather and respond to feedback
  • identify security threats
  • make improvements

What we do with your data

If you complete the survey, we will share your data in an anonymised form with MNOs and other government departments, agencies and local authorities, as required.

We may share anonymised data about visits to gov.uk/alerts with other government departments, agencies and public bodies.

We may also share anonymised data with our technology suppliers, for example our hosting provider and the MNOs.

We will not:

  • sell or rent your data to third parties
  • share your data with third parties for marketing purposes
  • use your data in analytics

We will share your data if we are required to do so by law - for example, by court order, or to prevent fraud or other crime.

Our legal basis for processing your data

The legal basis for sending emergency alerts is the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) 2003.

The PECR says that some public authorities can ask MNOs to send an emergency alert if an emergency:

  • has happened
  • is happening
  • is about to happen

An ‘emergency’ is defined in section 1 of the Civil Contingencies Act 2004.

The legal basis for processing personal data for gov.uk/alerts is our legitimate interests, and the legitimate interests of our users, in ensuring the security and integrity of our site.

The legal basis for processing survey data is consent. By completing the survey you are consenting for us to process any personal data you provide.

The legal basis for processing all other personal data is that it’s necessary:

  • to perform a task in the public interest
  • in the exercise of our functions as a government department

How long we keep your data

We will only retain your personal data for as long as:

  • it is needed for the purposes set out in this document
  • the law requires us to

We will delete personal data for gov.uk/alerts after 1 year.

We will keep feedback survey data according to our user research privacy notice.

Children’s privacy protection

Our services are not designed for, or intentionally targeted at, children 13 years of age or younger. We do not intentionally collect or maintain data about anyone under the age of 13.

Where your data is processed and stored

We design, build and run our systems to make sure that your data is as safe as possible at all stages, both while it’s processed and when it’s stored.

All personal data is stored in the UK and the European Economic Area (EEA).

How we protect your data and keep it secure

We are committed to doing all that we can to keep your data secure. We have set up systems and processes to prevent unauthorised access or disclosure of your data - for example, we protect your data using varying levels of encryption.

We also make sure that any third parties that we deal with keep all personal data they process on our behalf secure.

Your rights

You have the right to request:

  • information about how your personal data is processed
  • a copy of that personal data
  • that anything inaccurate in your personal data is corrected immediately

You can also:

  • raise an objection about how your personal data is processed
  • request that your personal data is erased if there is no longer a justification for it
  • ask that the processing of your personal data is restricted in certain circumstances

If you have any of these requests, get in contact with the GDS Privacy Team.

Links to other websites

This privacy notice only applies to gov.uk/alerts. It does not cover:

  • the rest of the gov.uk domain
  • other government services and transactions
  • any other websites that we link to

Other government services have their own terms and conditions and privacy policies.

If you go to another website from this one, read the privacy policy on that website to find out what it does with your information.
