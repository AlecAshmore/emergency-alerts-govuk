How Emergency Alerts works

Emergency Alerts works like a radio broadcast.

During an emergency, mobile phone masts in the surrounding area will broadcast an alert. Every compatible mobile phone or tablet in range of a mast will receive the alert.

The emergency services will not need your phone number to send you an alert.

You’ll get alerts based on your current location - not where you live or work.

Emergency Alerts are free and you will not need to sign up for them or download an app.

No one will collect or share data about you, your phone or your location when you receive an alert.

Your phone can still receive alerts if you’re on a call.

You will not get alerts while your phone or tablet is turned off or in aeroplane mode.

You can opt out of Emergency Alerts, but we recommend that you keep them switched on for your own safety.

Phone handsets and devices

Make sure your device has all the latest software updates.

Emergency Alerts work on:

  • iPhones and iPads running iOS 14.5 or later
  • Android phones and tablets running Android 11 or later

If you have an earlier version of Android, you may still be able to receive alerts. To check, search your settings for ‘Emergency Alerts’.

Mobile phone networks

Emergency Alerts work on all UK 4G and 5G phone networks.

Phones and tablets connected to a 2G or 3G network will not receive Emergency Alerts.

Emergency Alerts do not cause, and are not affected by, busy phone networks.
